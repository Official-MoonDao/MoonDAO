# 2026 Executive Lead Election — Path Options for Senate Review

**Status:** For Senate discussion and decision  
**Date:** July 2026  
**Related:** [Constitution §2.3.1 — Executive Leads](https://docs.moondao.com/Governance/Constitution)

---

## 1. Decision requested

The Senate is asked to decide **how to structure the 2026 Executive Lead election**, given that:

1. Pablo Moncada-Larrotiz’s first term concludes on **August 1, 2026**, and he is eligible to stand for a second (final) term.
2. MoonDAO may have a maximum of **three** Executive Leads at any time.
3. Today there are effectively **two** seated Executive Leads (Pablo and Ryan). The **third seat was not filled last year** (no nominations / no election outcome seating a lead).
4. A regular Executive Lead election occurs on a yearly basis under the Constitution.

**The core choice:** Should the competitive Member House race this cycle be for **Pablo’s seat**, or for the **open third seat** (with a separate confirmation / re-election path for Pablo)?

---

## 2. Background

| Fact | Detail |
|---|---|
| Max Executive Leads | 3 |
| Current seated leads | Pablo, Ryan |
| Open seat | Third Executive Lead seat (vacant since last year’s cycle) |
| Pablo’s term | Ends Aug 1, 2026; eligible for one more term (two-term maximum) |
| Election mechanics | Member nomination → Senate supermajority approval → Member House instant-runoff ranked choice, including **“none”** |
| Vacancy / remainder | If a seat is vacant mid-cycle, a special election may fill it; the winner serves the **remainder** of that term |

**Stagger intent:** Annual elections + three-year terms only produce orderly succession if seats are offset in time. Leaving the third seat empty for a full year is why a catch-up (shorter) term is on the table for that seat.

---

## 3. Option A — Competitive race for Pablo’s seat

**Structure:** Nominations and the Member House contest are framed as filling / renewing **Pablo’s Executive Lead seat**. Challengers run against the founder (or “none”). The third seat remains vacant unless addressed in a later cycle.

### Pros

- **Constitutionally straightforward.** Term ends → regular re-election for that seat. No need to explain a shortened term or dual ballots.
- **Clearest personal mandate.** A win is an explicit community renewal of Pablo’s second term; a loss or “none” is an unambiguous signal.
- **Single race, lower process load.** One nomination window, one Senate slate, one Member House ballot, one Q&A.

### Cons

- **Likely a no-op as a competitive process.** Running against the founder of the organization will deter many strong candidates. The field may be thin, and the outcome may be a foregone conclusion — accountability in form more than in substance.
- **Locks in a two-lead Executive for ~two more years.** If we only contest Pablo’s seat and leave the third seat empty, leadership stays at Ryan + Pablo. The third seat would only open in a meaningful “successor training” window roughly **a year before Pablo’s final term ends**, leaving about **one year** to onboard and train a successor — thin for institutional continuity.
- **Rapid succession risk if a challenger wins.** Though unlikely, a challenger victory (or “none” followed by scramble) would force a very fast handoff and risk a sharp loss of institutional knowledge.
- **Does not advance the Constitution’s three-lead design.** The original intent of up to three elected leads stays deferred.
- **Weaker pipeline for future leadership.** People who would compete for an *open* seat may not want to campaign against the founder, so the DAO learns less about who is ready to lead.

### Outcome sketch (Option A)

| Result | Effect |
|---|---|
| Pablo wins | Second term; still two leads; third seat still empty |
| Challenger wins | Rapid succession; Pablo out; still likely only two leads unless a separate fill is run |
| “None” wins | Pablo’s seat vacant; high operational risk |

## 4. Option B — Competitive race for the open third seat + confirmation of Pablo’s second term

**Structure (proposed):**

1. **Confirmation / re-election for Pablo’s seat** — a clear Member House (and Senate-approved) vote on whether Pablo is reinstated for a second term.
2. **Competitive election for the open third seat** — nominations and campaigning focused on who should *join* as the third Executive Lead.
3. **Seat assignment rule:**
   - If Pablo is **reinstated** → the election winner takes the **open third seat**.
   - If Pablo is **not** reinstated → the election winner takes **Pablo’s seat** (so the competitive race still produces a seated lead, and the DAO does not walk away empty-handed on succession).
4. **Term length for the open seat:** **2 years** (not 3), because there were **no nominations last year** and that seat’s cycle has already burned one year of the intended stagger. Framing: the winner serves the **remainder** of the vacant 2025–2028-style cycle so yearly elections stay staggered.
5. **Longer nomination period** for the open seat, so more people can step forward (including people who would not have run against the founder).
6. **Role / cost flexibility:** The third lead could begin as a more **governance-focused / part-time** role rather than full-time ops, with scope and compensation refined over time — but the DAO should assume some increase in burn rate.

### Pros

- **Smoother succession.** A third lead can learn the role, share external representation, and build context while Pablo and Ryan are still seated — instead of a one-year crash course at the end of Pablo’s final term.
- **Aligned with constitutional intent.** Moves toward the designed maximum of three elected Executive Leads, rather than operating indefinitely as a two-person lead group.
- **More real competition.** An open seat (not “unseat the founder”) is more likely to draw multiple serious nominees, especially with a longer nomination window.
- **Still includes a confirmation for Pablo.** Second-term legitimacy is not skipped; it is just separated from the competitive staffing question.
- **Built-in contingency.** If Pablo is not reinstated, the competitive winner can take his seat — avoiding a pure vacuum while still respecting the confirmation vote.
- **Restores election stagger.** A 2-year term for the formerly vacant seat is a practical catch-up so the third seat returns to the annual cadence instead of permanently drifting.

### Cons

- **Higher cost / burn rate.** One more person may take a salary (even if initially part-time or governance-weighted). Treasury and budget tradeoffs need an explicit Senate view.
- **More process complexity.** Two related questions (confirmation + open seat), clearer communications burden, and careful ballot design so members understand seat assignment rules.
- **Term-length interpretation.** A 2-year Executive Lead term is not spelled out as a standard length in the Constitution; it should be justified as **remainder of last year’s unfilled term / stagger repair**, not as inventing a new office class. Expect members to ask for this in writing.
- **Optics risk if poorly messaged.** Without clear framing, some may read Option B as “avoiding a real contest for Pablo’s seat.” The confirmation ballot and “none” option need to be real, visible, and fairly run.
- **Leadership bandwidth and Primus.** Three leads improve capacity but require clearer division of labor and a clean Primus Inter Pares process after the election.

### Outcome sketch (Option B)

| Pablo confirmation | Competitive race | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| Reinstated | Winner seated | Pablo (2nd term) + Ryan + new lead on **~2-year** open seat |
| Reinstated | “None” | Pablo + Ryan; third seat still vacant |
| Not reinstated | Winner seated | Winner takes Pablo’s seat; Ryan remains; third seat may still be empty depending on ballot design\* |
| Not reinstated | “None” | High risk — both confirmation and competitive path failed |

\*Senate should specify whether a failed Pablo confirmation + a winning candidate always assigns that winner to Pablo’s seat (recommended), and whether a *second* preference or follow-up is needed to still fill the third seat in the same season.

## 5. Side-by-side summary

| Dimension | Option A — Contest Pablo’s seat | Option B — Contest third seat + Pablo confirmation |
|---|---|---|
| Competitive signal | Weak (founder race) | Stronger (open seat) |
| Pablo second-term legitimacy | Via winning the competitive seat | Via explicit confirmation / re-election |
| # of leads next ~2 years | Likely stays at **2** | Can move to **3** |
| Succession / training runway | ~1 year before Pablo’s final step-down | Multi-year overlap with a third lead |
| Institutional knowledge risk if challenger wins | High (rapid succession) | Lower (smoother overlap); confirmation failure still disruptive but mediated by seated winner rule |
| Constitutional “three leads” intent | Deferred | Advanced |
| DAO burn rate | Unchanged on leadership headcount | Increases (mitigable via part-time / governance-first scope) |
| Process complexity | Lower | Higher |
| Term stagger | Unchanged problem (empty third seat) | Repaired via **2-year** remainder term |

## 6. Discussion questions for the Senate

### Ballot & process

1. **One season or two?** If Option B: are Pablo’s confirmation and the open-seat election on the **same** Member House timeline, or sequenced (confirmation first, then seat assignment)?
2. **What exact ballot text** do members see for Pablo’s confirmation? Binary reinstate / do not reinstate? Ranked options including “none”? Same IRV machinery as a multi-candidate race?
3. **If Pablo is not reinstated and there are multiple competitive candidates**, does the IRV winner automatically receive Pablo’s seat? Should runners-up be considered for the third seat in the same cycle?
4. **If Pablo is reinstated and “none” wins the open seat**, do we accept vacancy again, or schedule a follow-up special election within 60 days?
5. **Nomination window length** for Option B: how many extra days/weeks, and does Senate review stay on the published July calendar or slip?

### Constitutional & term design

6. Is the Senate comfortable treating the open seat’s **2-year term** as the **remainder** of last year’s unfilled cycle? Should that interpretation be recorded in the Senate vote / election announcement?
7. Does a 2-year remainder term require any **formal amendment**, or is election announcement + Senate approval of the slate sufficient for this one-time catch-up?
8. When the 2-year term ends, does that seat return to a normal **3-year** term at the next regular election?

### Role, pay, and operations

9. What is the **initial scope** of a third Executive Lead — full-time ops, part-time governance, or hybrid? Who proposes salary (individual lead right under the Constitution) and what budget envelope is acceptable?
10. How do we describe the third role externally so candidates know whether they are signing up for representation/governance vs day-to-day execution?
11. How will **Primus Inter Pares** (annual budget lead among Executive Leads) be handled immediately after seating a third lead?

### Succession & continuity

12. Beyond seating a third lead, what is the **explicit succession plan** for Pablo’s eventual final step-down (docs, access, external relationships, multisig/ops runbooks)?
13. Should Ryan’s term calendar and the new seat’s 2-year calendar be published side-by-side so the community can see the stagger for the next several years?
14. Are there contributors the Senate believes should be **encouraged to run** for an open seat who would not run against Pablo?

### Legitimacy & communications

15. How do we communicate Option B so it is understood as **stronger succession design**, not as shielding the founder from competition?
16. What does a healthy **“none”** campaign look like on both ballots, so vacancy remains a real constitutional outcome rather than a taboo?
17. Do election materials need a short **FAQ** (seat assignment rules, term length, salary expectations) before nominations close?

### Risk cases

18. **Minimum viable Executive:** What is the Senate’s contingency if confirmation fails and the competitive race also yields “none”?
19. **Conflict of interest / campaigning norms:** Any rules on use of official channels, Executive Branch resources, or founder platform during the race?
20. **Treasury signer interaction:** Does seating a third Executive Lead imply any near-term change to signer set, or is that a separate proposal later?

## 7. Considerations not fully settled by “A vs B” alone

These are adjacent decisions the Senate may want to bundle or explicitly defer:

- **Budget line:** Even a “governance-first” third lead needs a clear compensation and expense policy before candidates nominate in good faith.
- **Workload map:** Which responsibilities move from Pablo/Ryan to a third lead in the first 90 days (external comms, Senate liaison, project oversight, etc.)?
- **Timeline vs published election calendar:** Current public materials have framed the cycle around Pablo’s term end. Changing path may require updated nomination deadlines, Q&A framing, and Discord/Luma copy.
- **Historical record:** Last year’s empty third seat should be stated plainly in the announcement so the 2-year term is explained as catch-up, not preference.
- **Member House clarity:** Dual outcomes (confirmation + competitive seat) increase the chance of confusion; a one-page diagram of “if X then Y seat” is worth shipping with the vote.
- **Culture of challengers:** Long-term, the DAO may want a standing norm that **open seats are the default competitive race**, and incumbent renewals are confirmation-style — so future founders/leads are not the only viable path to leadership.

---

## 8. Suggested decision format

A Senate motion could take a form similar to:

> The Senate selects **Option [A / B]** as the structure for the 2026 Executive Lead election.  
> If Option B: (i) Pablo’s seat shall be subject to a confirmation / re-election vote; (ii) the competitive election shall be for the open third Executive Lead seat with a **2-year** term reflecting the remainder of last year’s unfilled cycle; (iii) if Pablo is not reinstated, the competitive winner is seated in Pablo’s seat; (iv) nomination window and candidate role/compensation guidance shall be published with the election announcement.

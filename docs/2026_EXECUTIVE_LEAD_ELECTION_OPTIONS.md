# 2026 Executive Lead Election — Options for Senate Review

**Status:** For Senate decision · July 2026  
**Ref:** [Constitution §2.3.1](https://docs.moondao.com/Governance/Constitution)

## Decision

How should we structure this year’s Executive Lead election?

**Facts:** Max 3 Executive Leads. Currently seated: Pablo + Ryan. Third seat vacant (no nominations last year). Pablo’s first term ends **Aug 1, 2026**; he is eligible for a second (final) term. Elections are yearly; “none” is a valid outcome.

**Core choice:** Compete for **Pablo’s seat**, or for the **open third seat** (with a separate confirmation vote for Pablo)?

---

## Option A — Compete for Pablo’s seat

Challengers run against the founder (or “none”). Third seat stays empty unless filled later.

**Pros**
- Constitutionally simple: term ends → re-election for that seat
- Clear personal mandate (win / lose / “none”)
- One race, less process

**Cons**
- Likely a **no-op** — few strong candidates will run against the founder
- Leaves only **Ryan + Pablo** for ~two more years; the third seat would only open ~**one year** before Pablo’s final step-down — thin runway to train a successor
- If a challenger wins (unlikely), **rapid succession** and sharp loss of institutional knowledge
- Defers the Constitution’s intent of **three** elected leads

---

## Option B — Compete for the open third seat + confirm Pablo

1. **Confirmation vote** on Pablo’s second term  
2. **Competitive race** for the open third seat (longer nomination window so more people can run)  
3. **Seat assignment:** if Pablo is reinstated → winner gets the open seat; if not → winner gets Pablo’s seat  
4. **Term for the open seat: 2 years** (not 3), because there were no nominations last year — framed as the remainder of that unfilled cycle so elections stay staggered  
5. Third lead could start as a **governance / part-time** role rather than full-time ops; scope and pay can evolve

**Pros**
- **Smoother succession** — overlap to train a future lead instead of a one-year crash course
- Matches constitutional intent of **three** elected leads
- More real competition (open seat, not “unseat the founder”)
- Pablo still faces a confirmation vote for his second term
- Built-in contingency if confirmation fails
- Restores election stagger via the 2-year remainder term

**Cons**
- **Higher burn rate** (another salary, even if part-time at first)
- More process / clearer ballot messaging required
- 2-year term needs a clear “remainder of last year’s vacant cycle” explanation
- Poor messaging could look like shielding the founder — confirmation + “none” must be real

---

## Summary

| | Option A (Pablo’s seat) | Option B (third seat + confirmation) |
|---|---|---|
| Competition | Weak (vs founder) | Stronger (open seat) |
| # of leads next ~2 years | Likely **2** | Can be **3** |
| Successor training runway | ~1 year | Multi-year overlap |
| Three-lead intent | Deferred | Advanced |
| Burn rate | Unchanged | Increases (mitigable) |
| Process | Simpler | More complex |

---

## Discussion questions

1. Same timeline for confirmation + open-seat race, or sequenced?
2. Exact confirmation ballot: binary, or IRV including “none”?
3. If Pablo is not reinstated, does the competitive winner automatically take his seat? Any path to still fill the third seat same cycle?
4. Is the Senate comfortable with a **2-year remainder term** without a constitutional amendment? When that term ends, does the seat return to a normal 3-year term?
5. Initial scope and budget for a third lead — full-time, part-time, or governance-first? Acceptable burn?
6. Contingency if confirmation fails **and** the competitive race yields “none”?
7. How do we communicate Option B as succession design, not founder protection?
8. Do we publish an updated seat calendar (Ryan / Pablo / new seat) so the stagger is visible?

---

## Suggested motion

> The Senate selects **Option [A / B]** for the 2026 Executive Lead election.  
> If Option B: (i) Pablo’s seat gets a confirmation / re-election vote; (ii) the competitive race is for the open third seat with a **2-year** term (remainder of last year’s unfilled cycle); (iii) if Pablo is not reinstated, the competitive winner takes Pablo’s seat; (iv) nomination window and role/compensation guidance are published with the election announcement.
